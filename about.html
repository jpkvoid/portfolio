Julien Palluel Kochnitzky is a pianist, composer, multimedia artist and creative programmer born in 1994 in Lausanne, Switzerland. After taking classical piano lessons at the Lausanne Conservatoire, he starts his professional music studies at the jazz and pop music school EJMA, still in Lausanne, in the preparatory course for music Bachelor. After two years he goes on to start his Bachelor in jazz piano at the Royal Conservatoire of the Hague, Netherlands, where he improves on his instrumental technique and his composition and arranging skills.

He graduates from his Bachelor in July 2022 with a minor in Sonology, where he studied electronic music through audio programming with classes such as Live Processes with Max MSP or Algorithmic Composition. With theses new skills he takes part in sound installations and starts to create visual and audio-visual algorithmic works. At the same time he starts to program his own synthesizers and now mainly plays his own instruments (if not the piano) in live and studio settings.

Throughout the years and projects he plays on many stages such as: Montreux Jazz Festival, Festi'Neuch, Cully Jazz Festival, Label Suisse, Prémices Festival, La Spirale, to name a few. He also composes and plays for two projects in collaboration with the Musée de l'Élysée, Lausanne's photography museum, writing pieces for and played in front of some of the works of their exhibitions.

Oscillating between Switzerland and the Netherlands, his projects range from Etienne Machine's electornic rock to his own project based on contemporary jazz, tinted by electronic music and for which he writes his own programs that are at the core of the compositions.

He enrolls in the Sonology Department of the Royal Conservatoire of the Hague starting in September 2022 to deepen his knowledge of electronic music, studying the works of the masters such as Karlheinz Stockhausen, Iannis Xenakis or Pierre Schaeffer.
